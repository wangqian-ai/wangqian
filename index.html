Biography

Lecturer

School of Art and Design, GuangDong University of Technology

Qian Wang is a lecturer at the School of Art and Design, GuangDong University of Technology. Her academic journey and professional pursuits revolve around the intersection of computer vision, AI painting, and computational art.

She earned her Ph.D. in Computer Vision from Macau University of Science and Technology. Post - doctoral research experience at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University further honed her expertise. With a unique interdisciplinary perspective, she bridges the realms of technology and art, delving into AI - generated artwork, image processing, and educational technology.

In the academic sphere, her contributions are notable. She has published research in prestigious journals such as ACM Comput. Surv. and IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems. Her work encompasses cutting - edge topics, from developing AI painting methodologies to advancing image deblurring techniques.

Additionally, she holds patents in innovative areas like AI painting and human posture recognition, reflecting her commitment to translating research into practical applications.

Beyond research, Qian Wang is dedicated to nurturing the next generation of talent. As a lecturer, she imparts knowledge and inspires students to explore the fascinating blend of art and technology. Her journey is marked by a continuous quest to push the boundaries of what’s possible in the digital creative landscape, making her a valuable contributor to both academic and practical domains of computer - related art and technology.

Qian Wang's research interests include computer vision, AI painting, and computational art.

1. Computer Vision

Focuses on AI-generated artwork, image processing, and educational technology applications. Her work explores the intersection of technology and art, particularly in developing AI painting methodologies and image deblurring techniques.

Guangdong University of Technology Young 100 Talents Program（广东工业大学青年百人项目）

+

PI (AI Painting Algorithm Optimization，2023 - present，￥100,000)

The wide proliferation of various AI tools and artistic algorithms has fueled the exploration of AI painting. In this project, we focus on optimizing stroke-by-stroke painting logic to mimic human creativity. Key stages include: Style Analysis, Stroke Simulation, and Creative Refinement.

National Natural Science Foundation（国家自然科学基金项目）

+

Co-PI (Research on Key Technologies of Object Segmentation for Vascular Interventional Surgery Robots, 2024 - 2027, supported by National Natural Science Foundation Youth Fund)

Integrates AI to assist digital art creation, featuring Intelligent Brush Suggestions, Composition Optimization, and Style Adaptation. It simplifies complex artistic techniques, supported by the School of Art and Design.

Image Deblurring for Art Restoration

+

Co-PI (2022 - 2024, supported by Guangdong Art Innovation Fund)

Leveraging computer vision techniques, this project aims to restore blurred digital artworks. Research includes: Noise Reduction, Adaptive Deblurring, and Artistic Style Preservation. Funded by Guangdong Art Innovation Fund, it bridges technology and cultural heritage preservation.

2. AI Painting

Investigates the integration of AI in creative processes, with a focus on human-like logic in stroke-by-stroke painting and computational art theory.

National Natural Science Foundation（国家自然科学基金项目）

+

PI (LogicStrokePainter: AI Human-like Painting Research，2025 - 2027, ￥300,000, supported by National Natural Science Foundation Youth Fund)

This project explores human-like painting logic using AI, focusing on: Creative Decision Modeling, Context-Aware Stroke Generation, and Interactive Art Refinement. It aims to bridge the gap between computational efficiency and artistic creativity.

AI-Generated Art Curation System

+

Co-PI (2024 - 2026, supported by Guangdong University of Technology Innovation Project)

Focuses on curating AI-generated artworks using Style Clustering, Thematic Tagging, and User Preference Modeling. It supports artists in discovering and refining creative directions, funded by Guangdong University of Technology.

3. Computational Art

Explores the use of computational tools to enhance artistic expression, including digital art creation frameworks and artistic style transfer.

Computational Art Style Transfer Platform

+

PI (2023 - 2025, supported by Guangdong Cultural and Creative Project)

Develops a platform for artistic style transfer using Deep Neural Networks, Feature Extraction, and Real-time Rendering. It empowers artists to experiment with cross-style creations, funded by Guangdong Cultural and Creative Project.

AI-Assisted Digital Art Creation Tool

+

Co-PI (2022 - 2024, supported by School of Art and Design Innovation Fund)

Integrates AI to assist digital art creation, featuring Intelligent Brush Suggestions, Composition Optimization, and Style Adaptation. It simplifies complex artistic techniques, supported by the School of Art and Design.
